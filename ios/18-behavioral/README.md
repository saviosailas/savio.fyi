# 18. Behavioral & Leadership Questions

> As a senior developer with 7.5 years of experience, interviewers expect leadership, mentoring, and decision-making stories. Use the STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result.

---

## Q1: Tell me about yourself

**Structure your answer:**
1. Current role and key responsibilities (30 seconds).
2. Relevant experience highlights (30 seconds).
3. Why you're interested in this role (15 seconds).

**Example framework:**
> "I'm a senior iOS developer with about 7.5 years of experience, 6.6 of which have been focused on iOS. In my current role at [Company], I lead the iOS team on [product], where I've been responsible for [key achievement]. I've worked across the full iOS stack — from UIKit to SwiftUI, architecting features, mentoring junior developers, and driving our migration to modern concurrency. I'm excited about this role because [specific reason tied to the company]."

---

## Q2: Describe a technically challenging problem you solved

**Prepare 2-3 stories covering:**
- Performance optimization (e.g., reducing app launch time, fixing memory leaks).
- Architecture decision (e.g., migrating from MVC to MVVM, modularization).
- Debugging a complex issue (e.g., race condition, intermittent crash).

**STAR format:**
- Situation: What was the context?
- Task: What was your specific responsibility?
- Action: What did you do? (Be specific about technical decisions)
- Result: What was the measurable outcome?

---

## Q3: How do you handle disagreements with team members?

**Key points to convey:**
- You listen first and seek to understand their perspective.
- You focus on data and technical merit, not ego.
- You're willing to prototype both approaches if needed.
- You commit to the team decision even if it wasn't your preference.
- You escalate constructively when needed.

---

## Q4: How do you mentor junior developers?

**Talk about:**
- Code reviews — constructive feedback with explanations.
- Pair programming sessions.
- Gradually increasing responsibility.
- Creating documentation and coding guidelines.
- Encouraging them to present in team meetings.

---

## Q5: Tell me about a time you had to make a trade-off

**Common iOS trade-offs:**
- Ship faster vs. perfect architecture.
- UIKit (stable, known) vs. SwiftUI (modern, some limitations).
- Third-party library vs. building in-house.
- Performance vs. code readability.

**Show that you:**
- Identified the trade-off clearly.
- Evaluated options with the team.
- Made a decision with reasoning.
- Documented the decision for future reference.

---

## Q6: How do you stay current with iOS development?

- WWDC sessions and release notes.
- Swift Evolution proposals.
- iOS developer blogs (Swift by Sundell, Hacking with Swift, etc.).
- Open source contributions or personal projects.
- Community (meetups, conferences, Twitter/X).
- Experimenting with new APIs in side projects.

---

## Q7: Describe your experience with Agile/Scrum

**Be ready to discuss:**
- Sprint planning and estimation.
- Daily standups — how you communicate blockers.
- Code reviews and PR process.
- Retrospectives — how you've improved team processes.
- Working with product managers and designers.

---

## Q8: Why are you leaving your current role?

**Good answers (keep it positive):**
- Seeking new technical challenges.
- Want to work on a product that [specific interest].
- Looking for growth opportunities in [area].
- Excited about the company's mission/product.

**Never say:** Bad management, boredom, conflicts.

---

## Q9: Where do you see yourself in 3-5 years?

**For a senior iOS developer:**
- Staff/Principal Engineer — deep technical leadership.
- Engineering Manager — people leadership.
- Architect — system-wide technical decisions.

Show that you have ambition but are also committed to growing in the role you're applying for.

---

## Q10: Do you have questions for us?

**Always ask questions. Good ones:**
- What does the iOS team structure look like?
- What's the tech stack? UIKit, SwiftUI, or both?
- How do you handle technical debt?
- What does the release process look like?
- What's the biggest technical challenge the team is facing?
- How do you support professional development?
- What does success look like in this role in the first 6 months?
